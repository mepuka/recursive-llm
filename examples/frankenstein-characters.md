# Complete List of Named Characters in Frankenstein

## 1. Agrippa, Cornelius
**Relationships:** Historical figure; no direct relationships to other characters
**Role:** Medieval alchemist whose works Victor studied in his youth before abandoning them for modern science; represents Victor's early fascination with occult natural philosophy

## 2. Beaufort, Mr.
**Relationships:** Father of Caroline Beaufort; friend of Alphonse Frankenstein
**Role:** Proud merchant who fell into poverty and moved to Lucerne; died in his daughter's arms after Caroline nursed him for ten months; his death brought Caroline and Alphonse together

## 3. Clerval, Henry
**Relationships:** Victor Frankenstein's closest friend from childhood; son of a narrow-minded trader
**Role:** Victor's devoted companion who nurses him through illness; studies oriental languages; provides emotional support and represents imagination and poetry; eventually murdered by the Creature

## 4. Creature, The (also called Daemon/Monster/Fiend/Wretch)
**Relationships:** Created by Victor Frankenstein; murderer of William Frankenstein, Henry Clerval, and Elizabeth Lavenza
**Role:** Victor's animated creation; becomes vengeful after rejection and abandonment; demands a female companion; carries out revenge by killing Victor's loved ones; narrates portions of the story

## 5. De Lacey (Old Man)
**Relationships:** Father of Felix and Agatha De Lacey; former Parisian gentleman
**Role:** Blind exile living in German cottage; shows kindness to the Creature during their brief encounter before being driven away by his children's horror

## 6. De Lacey, Agatha
**Relationships:** Daughter of De Lacey; sister of Felix De Lacey
**Role:** Gentle, kind woman living in exile with her family; the Creature observes her and learns human behavior; flees in terror upon seeing the Creature

## 7. De Lacey, Felix
**Relationships:** Son of De Lacey; brother of Agatha; lover/husband of Safie
**Role:** Noble young man who helped Safie's father escape from prison; lives in exile as consequence; the Creature learns language and ethics by observing him; violently drives the Creature away from the cottage

## 8. Frankenstein, Alphonse
**Relationships:** Victor's father; husband of Caroline Beaufort; father of Victor, Ernest, and William
**Role:** Respected Geneva magistrate; friend of Beaufort; married Caroline after Beaufort's death; supportive but unable to understand Victor's anguish; dies of grief after Elizabeth's murder

## 9. Frankenstein, Caroline (nee Beaufort)
**Relationships:** Daughter of Mr. Beaufort; wife of Alphonse Frankenstein; mother of Victor, Ernest, and William; adoptive mother of Elizabeth
**Role:** Found and adopted Elizabeth; devoted mother; nursed Elizabeth through scarlet fever; died of scarlet fever herself; on deathbed expressed wish for Victor and Elizabeth to marry

## 10. Frankenstein, Ernest
**Relationships:** Son of Alphonse and Caroline; brother of Victor and William
**Role:** Middle Frankenstein son; mentioned as younger sibling who needed care after mother's death; survives the tragedies

## 11. Frankenstein, Victor
**Relationships:** Son of Alphonse and Caroline; brother of Ernest and William; cousin/fiance of Elizabeth; friend of Henry Clerval; creator of the Creature
**Role:** Protagonist and primary narrator; studies natural philosophy at Ingolstadt; creates and abandons the Creature; pursues the Creature to the Arctic after it murders his loved ones; dies aboard Walton's ship

## 12. Frankenstein, William
**Relationships:** Youngest son of Alphonse and Caroline; brother of Victor and Ernest
**Role:** Beloved youngest Frankenstein child; murdered by the Creature near Geneva; his death is falsely attributed to Justine Moritz

## 13. Kirwin, Mr.
**Relationships:** Irish magistrate; no family relationships to main characters
**Role:** Investigates Henry Clerval's murder; initially suspects Victor; shows kindness during Victor's illness and imprisonment in Ireland

## 14. Krempe, M. (Professor)
**Relationships:** University professor at Ingolstadt
**Role:** Victor's natural philosophy professor; makes harsh but accurate assessments of Victor's progress; gives blunt compliments

## 15. Lavenza, Elizabeth
**Relationships:** Adopted cousin/sister of Victor Frankenstein; daughter of a Milanese nobleman and German mother; becomes Victor's fiancee; ward of the Frankenstein family
**Role:** Victor's childhood companion and eventual bride; embodies goodness and beauty; nursed Caroline through scarlet fever; murdered by the Creature on her wedding night

## 16. Magnus, Albertus
**Relationships:** Historical figure; no direct relationships
**Role:** Medieval philosopher whose works Victor studied alongside Agrippa and Paracelsus before abandoning for modern science

## 17. Moritz, Justine
**Relationships:** Servant/ward of the Frankenstein family; daughter of Madame Moritz
**Role:** Beloved family servant who lived with the Frankensteins; falsely accused and executed for William's murder after the Creature plants evidence on her; her death deepens Victor's guilt

## 18. Moritz, Madame
**Relationships:** Mother of Justine Moritz
**Role:** Treated Justine cruelly; Justine left her care to live with the Frankensteins

## 19. Paracelsus
**Relationships:** Historical figure; no direct relationships
**Role:** Alchemist whose works Victor studied in youth; represents Victor's early unscientific interests before his university education

## 20. Safie
**Relationships:** Daughter of a Turkish merchant; lover/wife of Felix De Lacey; her mother was a Christian Arab
**Role:** Arabian woman who travels to join Felix; the Creature learns language by observing her lessons; her story illustrates themes of prejudice and exile; her father's trial led to the De Lacey family's misfortunes

## 21. Saville, Margaret (Mrs.)
**Relationships:** Sister of Robert Walton; lives in England
**Role:** Recipient of Walton's letters that frame the narrative; expressed concerns about his Arctic expedition; audience for the story

## 22. Thomas, Uncle
**Relationships:** Uncle of Robert Walton
**Role:** Deceased; owned library of voyage histories that inspired young Walton; was forbidden by Walton's dying father from allowing Walton to pursue seafaring

## 23. Waldman, M. (Professor)
**Relationships:** University professor at Ingolstadt; mentor to Victor
**Role:** Chemistry professor whose lecture inspired Victor to pursue his fatal experiments; praised modern science's achievements; unintentionally encouraged Victor's obsessive research

## 24. Walton, Robert
**Relationships:** Brother of Margaret Saville; nephew of Uncle Thomas; Arctic explorer
**Role:** Frame narrator; rescues Victor in the Arctic; writes letters to his sister describing Victor's story; witness to Victor's death and encounter with the Creature; represents Romantic ambition similar to Victor's
